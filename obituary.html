A Life of Tender Mercies
In Memoriam

Dee Merlin Stevens

November 3, 1950  —  May 22, 2026
✦

They say lightning cracked the sky on November 3rd, 1950 when Dee Merlin Stevens entered this world. And that was just the start. Comparable only to the great mythical gods of Olympus, Dee carried an electrifying zeal for God, music, fishing, and family.

A devout follower of Jesus, Dee spent two years as a full-time ambassador for the Lord in the New York Cumorah Mission in the late 60s. Legend has it that every living person in a 25-mile radius of the old Cumorah Mission can trace their spiritual awakening to the slender missionary with a bounce in his step and a song on his lips.

Through his early college years, he buried his father, married, and started a family before terrible family circumstances forced him away from school and into the workforce.

After his first marriage ended, Dee found himself back on the market and — despite becoming a leading candidate to be the next Levi's denim model — he chose a much richer path when he met and married Kathy, who also had three boys of her own. Worried that their small home would feel too empty with only seven children, Dee and Kathy had two more, both boys.

The goal was always to return to school at some point and become a teacher, but Dee's secret identity as an industrious mastermind soon launched his career into management levels, where he'd spend years at various grocery stores, taking them from the brink of bankruptcy to the top of the market. Other grocery store managers would often stop by just to watch him work.

The Savant of Smith's. The Amethyst of Albertson's. The Knight of Non-Foods.

A hero of this type cannot be forged in the faint flicker of ease and prosperity, however, and so it was that Dee, the Deliverer of Deli, the Boss of Baked Goods, was attacked in a ruthless and cowardly fashion by his kidneys. They died, and tried everything to drag Dee down with them.

But they did not know the man against whom they conspired.

This inner revolt nearly claimed his life, but like the phoenix rising from its ashes, and with a new cooperating kidney, Dee set his sights on a future he long ago dreamed of.

And so it was, within a couple of years, the one-time Fanatic of Frozen Goods emerged from his cocoon to spread his new wings as a Middle School Social Studies Teacher. For nearly a decade, he bestowed his age-old knowledge upon many students, fulfilling a profound and sacred calling that had stirred within him since the lightning cracked the sky.

But the mortal vessel is a temporary host, and it gives out long before the soul is ready. Only this time, it was not Dee's, but that of his beloved Kathy, purveyor of chocolate cake and patron saint of selflessness. She crossed back into the realm of abundance and light only three years ago.

Since that time, Dee committed his powers to discovering and serving his ancestors until his body could no longer contain the light he had gathered.

And so, not with the crack of lightning, but with a gentle roll of thunder did Dee Merlin Stevens cross to the other side from whence he came, undoubtedly greeted with fanfare and love from those who have been forever changed by his insatiable drive for right.

"She is not gone. She is ahead. I intend to catch up."
