A note before we begin

Who this is for,
and why it exists

My name is Dee Stevens. I was born in 1950, raised in Utah County, and have lived in more places than I can count. I have been a missionary, a grocery store manager, a middle school teacher, a father of eight sons, a caretaker, and a widower. I have had both my kidneys removed and survived on a borrowed one for twenty-five years. I have buried children and parents and a wife I loved for forty-two years.

I am writing this down because I want my grandchildren and great-grandchildren to know who I was — not just what I did. I want them to know what I believed, what broke me, and what put me back together every time.

The title comes from a phrase I have returned to throughout my life. A tender mercy is what I call it when the Lord steps into an ordinary moment in a way that is unmistakably personal — not a grand public miracle, but something quiet and specific and meant for you. I have experienced more of these than I deserve, and I have tried to write down as many as I can remember, before the remembering gets any harder.

If you are one of my family, this is yours. If you are a stranger who found this somewhere — welcome. These things really happened. I was there for all of them.
